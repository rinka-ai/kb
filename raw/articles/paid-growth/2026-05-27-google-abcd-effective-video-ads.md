# Google Ads: About the ABCDs of effective video ads

## TL;DR

Google's ABCD framework is a compact video creative diagnostic: earn attention, make the brand clear, create an emotional or rational connection, and direct the viewer toward the next action.

## Key Claims

- The four principles are Attention, Branding, Connection, and Direction.
- Google describes the guidelines as data-backed and usable across campaign sizes.
- ABCD execution should change depending on awareness, consideration, and action objectives.
- Video specs and safe zones remain part of the execution layer.

## Important Details

- Attention is about getting noticed early.
- Branding is about making the advertiser memorable and clearly associated with the ad.
- Connection is about making people think or feel something.
- Direction is about clearly telling the viewer what to do next.
- Google cites Google/Kantar research tying ABCD adherence to short-term sales likelihood and long-term brand contribution.

## My Notes

- ABCD is a useful cross-platform creative review checklist, not only a YouTube artifact.
- It pairs well with UGC scripts: the hook earns attention, the creator/story builds connection, the product proof carries branding, and the CTA supplies direction.
