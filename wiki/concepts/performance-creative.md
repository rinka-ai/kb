# Performance Creative

## Summary

Performance creative is not decoration for media buying. In Meta and Google systems, creative is a primary optimization input. Automated delivery needs many coherent assets, but the assets must come from real customer insight, distinct angles, channel-native formats, and a measurement loop that produces new briefs.

## Creative Inputs

Useful creative starts before the design or shoot:

- voice-of-customer phrases
- buyer pain and desired outcome
- objections and anxieties
- competitor alternatives
- product mechanism or differentiator
- proof points and demonstrations
- social proof and credible endorsers
- policy constraints and claim boundaries

Without this layer, AI creative tools mostly multiply vague claims.

## Angle Matrix

A durable performance creative library should separate:

- problem angle
- desired outcome angle
- mechanism angle
- objection angle
- comparison angle
- proof angle
- urgency angle
- identity or role angle
- use-case angle
- testimonial angle

Each angle can become search headlines, social primary text, creator briefs, video hooks, overlays, static concepts, landing-page sections, and email copy.

## Asset System

Meta Advantage+ creative and Google Performance Max both want a broad asset supply. The system should include:

- square, vertical, and horizontal images
- vertical video for Reels/Shorts-style surfaces
- product demos and proof clips
- UGC and creator-led videos
- primary text and headline variants
- Google RSA headline and description modules
- YouTube/short-form scripts using Attention, Branding, Connection, Direction
- landing-page copy matched to ad promises

The goal is not raw volume. It is enough meaningful variation for the platform to learn what message, format, and proof type fits each context.

## Testing Loop

Performance creative should produce learning, not just winners.

Loop:

1. Mine research and performance history.
2. Write angle hypotheses.
3. Produce assets in channel-native formats.
4. Launch with clean naming and tracking.
5. Wait long enough to avoid premature kills.
6. Read results by angle, hook, format, offer, and funnel quality.
7. Refresh or remix winners.
8. Retire assets that are fatigued, non-compliant, or low quality.

## AI Role

AI can help generate variations, adapt formats, and speed production. Meta's AdLlama paper shows that platforms are training ad text systems with performance feedback, and Meta/Google both provide generative asset tools.

But AI does not replace:

- offer truth
- customer understanding
- legal review
- brand judgment
- landing-page match
- incrementality measurement

AI-generated assets should be treated as candidates, not truth.

## Tensions

- Platform recommendations often reward variation, but teams still need message control.
- The asset that wins CTR may not win qualified conversion or retention.
- UGC may outperform polished ads in cold feed contexts, but polished proof may be better for high-consideration offers.
- Too much creative churn can reset learning; too little refresh creates fatigue.
