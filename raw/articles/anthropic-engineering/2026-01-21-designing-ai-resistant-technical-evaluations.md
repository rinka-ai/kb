## TL;DR

Anthropic shows how quickly strong models can invalidate traditional take-home tests, and argues that robust evaluation design now has to assume candidates will have access to frontier AI assistance.

## Key Claims

- Technical evaluations can lose signal quickly as models improve.
- More realistic tasks are not automatically more AI-resistant.
- Out-of-distribution and tool-building components can help restore differentiation.

## Important Details

- The post describes three iterations of a performance-engineering take-home.
- Anthropic moved from a realistic optimization problem toward stranger puzzle-like tasks.
- The piece explicitly discusses the tension between realism and resistance to AI assistance.
