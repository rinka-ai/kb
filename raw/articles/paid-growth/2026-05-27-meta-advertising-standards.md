# Meta: Introduction to the Advertising Standards

## TL;DR

Meta ad creative is constrained by policy before it is constrained by performance. Ads, assets, text, targeting information, landing pages, and business behavior can be reviewed or re-reviewed, and violations can restrict both ads and business assets.

## Key Claims

- Meta reviews ads against Advertising Standards and Community Standards.
- The review process may inspect images, video, text, targeting information, landing pages, and destinations.
- Ads cannot use deceptive or misleading practices, assert sensitive personal attributes, or exploit negative self-perception in restricted categories.
- Ads can be rejected or re-reviewed after launch, and business assets can be restricted for policy problems.

## Important Details

- Meta's policy principles include protecting people from unsafe or discriminatory practices, fraud or scams, poor user experiences, and opacity.
- The personal attributes policy blocks direct or indirect assertions about traits such as health, disability, vulnerable financial status, voting status, religion, age, sexual orientation, and more.
- The health and wellness policy restricts weight-loss, cosmetic, and health-related content that implies negative self-perception or idealized body outcomes.
- Lead ads have additional restrictions around sensitive data collection.
- Advertisers are responsible for local laws and self-regulatory codes in addition to Meta's rules.

## My Notes

- This is the compliance anchor for every Meta creative playbook. Copywriting and UGC scripts should be reviewed for implied personal attributes and unrealistic claims before launch.
- A useful KB heuristic: if a line works by making the viewer feel diagnosed, exposed, ashamed, or guaranteed a result, it may be both bad trust-building and bad policy.
