# LinkedIn: User Agreement

## TL;DR

LinkedIn's current User Agreement makes the platform stance on automation unusually explicit. Scraping, browser-plugin automation, bot-driven messaging, and other unauthorized automated interactions are prohibited, so LinkedIn should not be treated as a carefree programmable outreach channel.

## Key Claims

- LinkedIn automation risk is a policy problem, not only a rate-limit problem.
- Unauthorized scraping and browser automation are directly restricted by the platform contract.
- Anti-spam compliance is part of the user's obligations on LinkedIn.
- Human review of AI-generated content is still required even for LinkedIn's own generative features.

## Important Details

- The current agreement is marked effective on November 3, 2025.
- In the "Dos," LinkedIn says users must comply with applicable laws, including anti-spam and privacy laws.
- In the "Don'ts," LinkedIn prohibits using software, devices, scripts, robots, crawlers, browser plugins, add-ons, or other means to scrape or copy the Services, including profiles and other data.
- The agreement also prohibits using bots or other unauthorized automated methods to access the Services, add or download contacts, send or redirect messages, or drive inauthentic engagement such as automated likes, comments, or shares.
- The generative-AI section says generated content may be inaccurate or misleading and should be reviewed and edited before sharing.
