## TL;DR

This is a high-signal practitioner artifact for AI-native content operations. Its reusable pattern is a human-in-the-loop pipeline: the creator owns the first mile of taste, direction, and context, then AI performs research, interviewing, drafting, review, repurposing, and learning-loop work, and the creator returns for final edit and approval. The strongest KB lesson is not "AI writes content"; it is that content can become a workflow of specialized skills, provenance-preserving raw material, critique loops, platform-native derivatives, and creator-specific memory.

## Key Claims

- A content system can be made AI-native by first mapping the historical human process rather than automating a vague task label.
- The human remains most important at the first and final mile: selecting the idea/direction, adding context, reviewing the final draft, and signing off.
- AI can handle much of the middle: idea discovery, research, interview prompting, raw material production, first drafts, first edits, derivative content, and performance tracking.
- Internal systems such as Slack, Notion, call transcripts, and Gmail can be mined for high-signal moments that deserve expansion into content.
- A sourced research report should precede interviews and should separate facts from opinions while checking claims adversarially.
- Interview quality depends on specificity: stories, numbers, emotional detail, and refusal to accept vague answers.
- The raw production file should preserve exact words before any polished draft abstracts them away.
- Drafting should use the creator's style guide, past feedback lessons, content-type spec, and raw stories or quotes.
- Expert-review personas can critique drafts through distinct lenses, but information gaps should route back to the creator or interview loop instead of being invented.
- A revision loop can continue until review scores reach a defined bar, here 9/10.
- One anchor artifact can become many platform-native derivatives if each derivative is re-hooked and reviewed on its own terms.
- A learning loop should compare first drafts with final approved versions and save confirmed lessons so future drafts improve.

## Important Details

- The system is explicitly described as a directory of skills that can run end-to-end or step-by-step.
- The idea pipeline has two input lanes: internal "spikes" from the creator's own work and an external feed of selected X accounts and websites.
- Qualifying ideas go into a Notion database called The Vault, with the human selecting from that queue.
- The Researcher creates `research-report.md` with TL;DR, sourced facts, current developments, prior discourse, contrarian angles, and open questions.
- The Interview Panel simulates several named interview styles and asks 12-15 questions one at a time.
- The Production step turns interviews into a raw Markdown file with transcript, key stories, insights, quotable moments, emotional anchor, surprising reveals, and the "so what."
- The Refinement step requires the content type, then combines style guide, past feedback, content-type spec, and raw material into a draft.
- The Writer's Council splits feedback into editorial changes the machine can make and information gaps only the creator can answer.
- The Repurposing Engine holds derivative assets to the same review and revision standard as the anchor content.
- The Learning Loop treats final human edits as training evidence for that creator, and says lessons override the style guide when they conflict.

The Content Machine — Alex Lieberman (@businessbarista)
Source: x.com/businessbarista/status/2062271970213863735 · Jun 3, 2026
Overview
A content pipeline that turned him into a one-person media company, drove tens of millions in pipeline for tenex_labs, is "allergic to AI-slop," and turned his employees into content creators. It's a directory of skills mimicking each step of his content process, run end-to-end or step-by-step.
Step 1: Map out the process
Make work AI-native by first understanding how it's been done historically (business context + domain expertise still matter). The traditional content process:

Look for inspiration
Pick a 10x content idea
Research the idea
Brain dump all thoughts about the idea
Decide the post format
Create a draft
Edit the post
Create derivative versions
Go live
Track performance

Step 2: Where am I needed vs. not needed?
Human (first & final mile):

First mile: picking the idea/direction + providing necessary context
Final mile: fine-tooth-comb review of final draft + final sign-off

AI (everything in between): inspiration, research, pulling thoughts out, writing, first edit, derivative content, performance tracking.
Step 3: Build the Content Machine
One pipeline, run end-to-end or step-by-step — a directory of skills mapping to the delegated steps.

The Oracle [AI] — Mines Slack, Notion, call transcripts, and Gmail for "spikes" (moments he naturally said something worth expanding). An Internet Reader curates an external feed of selected X accounts & websites. Qualifying ideas (≥6/10) get written to The Vault (a Notion database of content ideas).
Select the idea from The Vault [Human]
The Researcher [AI] — Before any interview, builds a sourced research-report.md: TL;DR, key facts with links, current developments, what's already been said, contrarian angles, open questions. Claims are adversarially checked; fact separated from opinion.
Interview Panel [AI + Human] — Six world-class interviewers (Joe Rogan, Howard Stern, Michael Barbaro, etc.) ask 12–15 questions, one at a time, each pushing a different dimension. Never satisfied with vague answers — won't advance without 2–3 specific stories, real numbers, and emotional specificity.
Production [AI] — Interview becomes a raw .md file: transcript, key stories, core insights, quotable moments, emotional anchor, surprising reveals, and the "so what." This raw file is sacred — exact words, never paraphrased away.
Refinement [AI + Human] — He tells the machine the content type. It reads his custom style guide + past feedback lessons + content-type spec, then drafts in his voice, pulling real stories/quotes from the raw file. #1 rule: write like you're texting a friend. Supports long posts, LinkedIn, X threads, and more.
Writer's Council [AI] — Six expert reviewers (Shaan Puri, Morgan Housel, David Perell, etc.) score the draft through their own lens, splitting fixes into editorial (machine can rewrite) and information gaps (only the creator can answer — these route back to the interview panel).
Revision Loop [AI] — Iterate until council scores 9/10.
Repurposing Engine [AI] — One anchor → 10+ natively-formatted derivatives, each re-hooked per platform and held to the same full Council → revision bar of 9/10. How two people produce like a hundred.
Final revision [Human]
Learning Loop [AI] — After approval, machine compares first draft vs. final, extracts confirmed lessons, saves to that creator's content-lessons.md. Every future first draft starts smarter. Lessons override the style guide when they conflict.
