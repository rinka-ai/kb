## TL;DR

Responsive search ads convert copywriting into modular headline and description assets. Google can mix up to 15 headlines and 4 descriptions to better match queries, devices, and user context.

## Key Claims

- Responsive search ads adapt to show more relevant messages to potential customers.
- More headlines and descriptions give Google more opportunities to assemble query-relevant ads.
- Assets can appear in different orders, so each line must make sense alone and in combination.
- Pinning gives control but reduces the system's flexibility.

## Important Details

- Advertisers can provide up to 15 headlines and 4 descriptions.
- Headline fields support up to 30 characters; description fields support up to 90 characters.
- Google tests combinations over time and learns which combinations are relevant to different queries.
- Pinning should generally be reserved for required language such as disclaimers, brand constraints, or legal claims.

## My Notes

- Search copy should be written like a set of modular Lego pieces, not like one fixed paragraph.
- The main copywriting constraint is intent clarity: every headline should either identify the category, express the value, overcome an objection, or push action.
