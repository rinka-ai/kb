# Google Ads

## Summary

Google Ads spans declared intent and algorithmic discovery. Search ads respond to explicit queries; Performance Max combines assets across Search, YouTube, Display, Discover, Gmail, Maps, and partner inventory. The advertiser's job is to provide coherent assets, clean conversion data, trustworthy destinations, and a measurement plan that distinguishes attributed results from incremental lift.

## Search Ads

Responsive search ads make copy modular. Advertisers provide headline and description assets, and Google assembles combinations for different queries and contexts.

Strong RSA asset sets include:

- category and keyword relevance
- core benefit
- proof or credibility
- offer or incentive
- objection handling
- location or urgency where genuine
- clear CTA
- brand or required disclaimer where needed

The key is semantic diversity. Fifteen headlines that repeat the same phrase give the system less real variation than eight headlines that each do a distinct job.

## Performance Max

Performance Max asset groups are thematic packs of text, images, logos, videos, URLs, and signals. Google recommends organizing asset groups around common themes, products, services, languages, or audiences.

Important asset principles:

- include enough text, image, logo, and video coverage
- create assets in multiple orientations
- avoid relying on auto-generated video when brand quality matters
- wait long enough before replacing low-performing assets
- use asset reports to guide replacement with variants similar to winners

Performance Max needs a source-of-truth website and landing-page system because text customization and Final URL expansion can use site content to match user intent.

## Video And YouTube

Google's ABCD framework is the video creative anchor:

- Attention: earn notice early
- Branding: make the brand clear and memorable
- Connection: make the viewer think or feel something
- Direction: tell the viewer what to do next

This works as a cross-platform review checklist for YouTube, Shorts, Reels, and other paid video formats.

## Content And Destination Quality

Google's people-first content guidance is not only SEO advice. Paid campaigns often send users to owned pages, and those pages need first-hand expertise, useful substance, clear purpose, and trust. Search-engine-first content that summarizes others, chases trends, or uses mass automation without added value weakens the paid path too.

## Policy And Trust

Google's misrepresentation policy makes ad-to-destination match a hard constraint. Risk rises when ads or pages hide charges, exaggerate results, promote unavailable offers, imply false affiliation, or omit key business details.

For paid growth, this means:

- the landing page must fulfill the ad promise
- claims must be substantiated
- business and pricing details should be visible where relevant
- AI-generated copy needs factual and policy review

## Measurement

Google Conversion Lift uses exposed and holdback groups to estimate incremental conversions, value, and ROAS. This matters because platform attribution is not the same as causal lift.

Google Ads teams should maintain three measurement layers:

- in-platform optimization metrics for day-to-day operations
- analytics and CRM data for funnel quality
- lift or geo experiments for causal budget decisions where feasible
