# Improving Generative Ad Text on Facebook using Reinforcement Learning

## Source Metadata

- Path: raw/articles/paid-growth/2026-05-27-adllama-generative-ad-text-facebook.md

## TL;DR

Meta's AdLlama paper shows where ad copy generation is heading: models trained not only to imitate human ads, but to optimize against historical performance feedback.

## Key Claims

- AdLlama powers a text generation feature for Facebook ads.
- The paper introduces reinforcement learning with performance feedback for post-training.
- Historical ad performance data is used as a reward signal.
- The authors report improved click-through rates versus a supervised imitation model in a large A/B test.

## Important Details

- The source matters because it explains why platform-native ad copy tools may increasingly optimize toward measured engagement or conversion proxies.
- It does not mean generated ad text is always strategically good, truthful, compliant, or brand-safe.
- Performance-feedback training can amplify patterns that work on-platform while still requiring advertiser governance.
- The advertiser's role shifts toward prompt inputs, brand constraints, claim substantiation, and review.
