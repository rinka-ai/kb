# AI Agency Sales Process

## Summary

Middleton's sales process is best preserved as a diagnostic sales call, not as a pressure script. The rep's job is to understand the buyer's funnel in numbers, show how the current strategy prevents the stated goal, then prescribe the acquisition system only when it fits. This makes the offer feel less like "AI automation" and more like a business-performance intervention.

The video calls it a seven-step process, but the discovery stage contains several substeps. The enriched KB version makes those substeps explicit so the process can become a usable call rubric and future [[sales-coaching-gpt]] input. The Pavlo interview adds the earlier-stage lesson that cold calling can train founder sales judgment, but it also shows why scripts need truthfulness, compliance, and pressure limits before becoming reusable SOPs.

## Call Flow

1. Intro and rapport: build a brief human connection, then move quickly into the business conversation.
2. Diagnose the funnel with numbers: leads, bookings, shows, closes, revenue, expenses, marketing spend, profitability, staffing, current tools, and recent trend.
3. Build doubt in the current approach: ask whether existing agencies, staff, or systems handle reviews, referrals, old leads, lead follow-up, sales coaching, AI usage, and ad spend decisions.
4. Identify goals: define the next 3-6 month target and ask whether the current path can realistically reach it.
5. Transition: summarize the situation, confirm nothing important is missing, and establish that the agency's expertise maps to the diagnosed problem.
6. Pitch the pillars: for each module, name the logical pain, the emotional/business consequence, the AI-enabled solution, the business benefit, and the exact diagnosed problem it solves.
7. Temperature check: ask how the buyer feels about the process before presenting price. If they are not at strong conviction, find the uncertainty before the close.
8. Close and next steps: outline agreement, payment, onboarding, setup, and success path before stating the investment.
9. Objection handling: sidestep the stated objection long enough to determine whether it is value uncertainty, trust, authority, timing, or real cash constraint.

## Diagnosis Questions

- How many leads did you get last month by source?
- How many booked appointments?
- How many showed up?
- How many became customers?
- How much did you spend on marketing and what revenue came back?
- What is average order value, LTV, gross margin, and current capacity?
- How quickly does your team respond to web leads and missed calls?
- What happens to no-shows, reschedules, and unclosed prospects?
- How often do you ask for reviews and referrals?
- Do you have a written sales process and regular coaching?

## Pitch Formula

For each pillar, use the same structure:

- Logical pain: the measurable gap found in discovery.
- Business consequence: what that gap costs in revenue, trust, time, or growth.
- Mechanism: the specific system being installed.
- Benefit: the outcome the buyer should expect if the gap is solved.
- Tie-down question: confirm they understand why this piece matters before moving on.

The formula prevents feature-dumping. The rep should not talk about reactivation, reviews, missed-call text-back, sales coaching, or ads until each one is connected to a gap the buyer already acknowledged.

## Qualification And Integrity

- Do not sell if the buyer lacks enough demand, margin, staff capacity, or operational trust to benefit.
- Do not use Middleton's reported close rates, lead costs, or client results as the agency's own proof.
- Do not use false-referral openers or invented customer relationships, even if a source claims they increased booking rates.
- Do not teach closers to force a credit card or a hang-up as the default close; track trust, cancellation, refund, and retention quality alongside close rate.
- Do not promise revenue, appointments, or ROI without niche-specific evidence and clear conditions.
- Treat "I need to think about it" as a signal to clarify uncertainty, not as permission to push harder.
- Keep a call scorecard so close rate improves through evidence rather than personality.

## Metrics

- Booked sales calls by source
- Show rate
- Qualified rate
- Close rate
- Average contract value and monthly recurring revenue
- No-decision reasons
- Lost-deal objections
- Onboarding completion rate
- Client activation and early churn

## Evidence And Authority

- Evidence status: this page is primarily extracted from Middleton's self-reported sales process. It is useful as an operator script, not as authoritative proof of close-rate performance.
- Sales strategy context: HBR's [The End of Solution Sales](https://hbr.org/2012/07/the-end-of-solution-sales) supports the idea that modern reps need more than feature presentation; they have to help buyers reframe problems and make a case for change.
- Sales operations evidence: [Salesforce State of Sales 2026](https://www.salesforce.com/en-us/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/documents/reports/sales/salesforce-state-of-sales-report-2026.pdf) is vendor survey evidence, but it supports the need for coaching, data quality, fewer tool silos, roleplay, and manager review in sales execution.
- Stronger evidence requirement: the agency should validate the process with its own call tracking, recorded call review, lead-source segmentation, close-rate cohorts, churn/refund outcomes, and post-sale client activation.
- Claim authority: sales claims must follow the same substantiation discipline as ad claims. Use [[2026-05-27-meta-advertising-standards]], [[copywriting]], and [[marketing-measurement]] as guardrails for proof, sensitive attributes, and outcome claims.
- Process authority: [[sales-coaching-gpt]] should become the rubric/evidence layer, but only after calibration against expert review.
