## TL;DR

Deloitte's survey supports the claim that enterprise AI investment is outpacing returns. The useful pattern is not "AI does not work"; it is that AI rarely delivers value in isolation from process integration, data improvements, and organizational redesign.

## Key Claims

- Most respondents report AI ROI timelines longer than the usual expected payback period for technology investments.
- Only a small minority report payback in under a year.
- AI value is difficult to isolate because it is often introduced alongside data quality, team, and operational changes.
- Agentic AI has greater complexity and may require end-to-end process redesign.
- Leading enterprises are becoming more selective about use cases and building structured programs for organizational change.

## Important Details

- The survey covers 1,854 executives across Europe and the Middle East, supported by 24 in-depth interviews.
- The article explicitly compares AI adoption to earlier general-purpose technology transitions that required redesigning workflows and infrastructure.
- It identifies siloed platforms, data quality issues, evolving metrics, and process integration as recurring ROI blockers.

## My Notes

- Strong source for treating AI as an operating-model transition rather than a plug-in software purchase.
- Useful caution: some value is intangible or confounded with other operational improvements, so ROI claims need careful measurement design.

## Open Questions
