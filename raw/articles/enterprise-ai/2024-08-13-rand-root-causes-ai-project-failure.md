# The Root Causes of Failure for Artificial Intelligence Projects and How They Can Succeed

## TL;DR

RAND is a strong source for the non-model failure thesis: AI projects fail when leaders choose the wrong problem, lack usable data, chase novelty, underinvest in infrastructure, or ask AI to solve problems it is not suited for.

## Key Claims

- More than 80 percent of AI projects fail by some estimates, at roughly twice the failure rate of non-AI IT projects.
- The most common causes in RAND's interviews were leadership and problem-framing failures.
- Data quality and data availability are persistent project blockers.
- Some projects fail because simple deterministic rules would have been cheaper and better than AI.
- AI infrastructure and deployment capability are recurring prerequisites for success.

## Important Details

- RAND interviewed 65 experienced data scientists and engineers from industry and academia.
- The report distinguishes technical failure from business failure.
- The industry recommendations emphasize domain context, enduring problems, problem-first design, infrastructure investment, and realism about AI limitations.

- Particularly useful support for the "do not throw everything at the LLM" failure mode.
