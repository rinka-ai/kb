## TL;DR

Voice-of-customer research turns customer language into conversion copy. The aim is not to invent clever phrasing, but to find how buyers describe their needs, frustrations, hesitations, and desired outcomes.

## Key Claims

- Voice-of-customer research captures customer needs in the customer's own words.
- Useful VoC work groups needs, prioritizes them, and segments them by audience or perceived benefit.
- Customer language can expose the messages needed to increase conversion and reduce friction.
- Surveys, interviews, reviews, forums, and on-site feedback can all provide copy inputs.

## Important Details

- The guide frames VoC as qualitative evidence that explains the "why" behind quantitative behavior.
- The copy task is to reflect the buyer's feelings and language back to them accurately.
- VoC should reveal motivations, pain points, hesitations, desired outcomes, and buying triggers.
- Research must be analyzed and prioritized, not pasted blindly into headlines.
