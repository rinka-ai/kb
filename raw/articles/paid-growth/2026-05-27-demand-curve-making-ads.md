## TL;DR

Good ad copy starts from what the audience already knows. Search ads can borrow explicit intent from the query; social ads often need more context because the person did not ask for the offer.

## Key Claims

- Copy should be written for a specific outcome such as selling, clicking, or converting.
- Social ads often require more context than search ads because audience intent is less explicit.
- The ideation process starts with product benefits and audience concerns.
- Strong pitches address why the problem matters, why now, why this solution, and what to do next.

## Important Details

- The guide highlights the "level of problem awareness" difference between search and paid social.
- It recommends listing value propositions and concerns before writing.
- It warns that copy must make the reader feel the problem enough to care, without becoming bloated.
- The process is compatible with voice-of-customer research, review mining, and audience interviews.
