## TL;DR

A broad optimization algorithms text useful for grounding ML training, model selection, and black-box search in concrete algorithm families.

## Key Claims

- Optimization is a search process over objective functions with local/global, constrained/unconstrained, and deterministic/stochastic variants.
- Derivative information changes the available algorithm family and convergence behavior.
- No single optimizer dominates; problem structure controls the right method.
- Surrogate and Bayesian optimization matter when objective evaluations are expensive.
