## TL;DR

The report is the closest hard-source match for the "enterprise AI is not working because workflows are not being transformed" thesis. It frames the failure mode as high adoption, low P&L impact, and poor workflow integration rather than inadequate model quality.

## Key Claims

- A small minority of integrated GenAI pilots produce material value, while most have no measurable P&L impact.
- The divide is not mainly driven by model quality, infrastructure, regulation, or talent.
- Task-specific enterprise tools often stall because they are brittle, lack contextual learning, and do not align with day-to-day operations.
- Generic tools can help individuals while still failing to transform enterprise workflows.
- External partnerships reportedly outperform internal builds in the report's sample.

## Important Details

- Methodology includes a review of more than 300 publicly disclosed AI initiatives, interviews with representatives from 52 organizations, and survey responses from 153 senior leaders.
- The report separates adoption from transformation: broad experimentation does not imply structural change.
- It highlights a "shadow AI" economy where employees use personal tools even while official initiatives stall.
- The strongest recommendation pattern is process-specific customization, business-outcome evaluation, and systems that improve from feedback.

## My Notes

- Strong support for treating enterprise AI failure as a systems and workflow problem.
- The exact headline numbers should be used with care because the report is a market research report with self-reported and public-observation components, not a controlled scientific study.
- Best used as a directional source for adoption gap, workflow brittleness, and the importance of learning loops.

## Open Questions

- Which kinds of "learning-capable systems" in the report correspond to durable memory, eval feedback, human correction loops, or process mining?
- How much of the 5 percent success claim depends on the report's definitions of "integrated pilot" and "measurable P&L impact"?
