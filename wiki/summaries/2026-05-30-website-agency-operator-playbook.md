# Website Agency Operator Playbook

## Summary

The Pavlo/Michael interview is useful because it fills a gap in the AI-agency notes: not just what a local-business acquisition system should contain, but how one operator says they actually got from zero to a scaled agency. The reported numbers are self-reported and should not be treated as audited proof. The durable lesson is the operating sequence: sell a low-risk wedge, learn sales through volume, add simple automations, build SOPs around founder behavior, hire around bottlenecks, and use AI only after the manual workflow is clear.

This source is also a warning. Some of the transcript's tactics are commercially effective but not acceptable as KB-approved scripts: a deliberately false referral opener, heavy pressure closes, cold SMS without visible compliance detail, and a review funnel described as preventing bad reviews. Preserve those as evidence of what the operator said, but do not copy them into approved implementation.

## Operator Pattern

- The wedge is cheap enough that skeptical local-business owners can try it: a website plus follow-up/reputation basics at $297/month is a softer ask than a $2k/month ads retainer.
- The offer gets more valuable through an upsell ladder: GBP/Maps fix, SEO, and Google Local Services Ads can move an account from $297/month toward $1k-$1.3k/month.
- Outbound is treated as skill acquisition, not only lead generation. High-volume cold calling produced sales reps who could later close Zoom calls, train closers, and evaluate scripts.
- Partnership worked because both operators were already proven: one brought marketing/public-face strength; the other brought systems, hiring, technical, and customer-success strength.
- Founder magic had to become process. A charismatic founder could close inconsistently at high rates, but the scalable version required scripts, training, roles, and scorecards.
- Customer success became the accountability center. The proposed future model gives one CSM A-to-Z responsibility for a client, with AI/software replacing separate VA handoffs.
- AI is a fulfillment compression tool after manual learning, not a starting point. The operator explicitly warns beginners not to copy the custom-coded AI delivery stack from day one.

## Agency Build Implications

- Start with a narrow first niche and a cheap, legible wedge before expanding into the full [[local-business-ai-acquisition-system]].
- Treat the first 100-500 outbound conversations as market research and sales training, but use compliant, truthful scripts.
- Filter lead lists earlier than the operator did: minimum review count, verified Google Business Profile, reachable owner/operator, active service area, and enough business maturity to pay.
- Build the upsell map on day one, even if upsells are sold later: base package, one-time GBP cleanup, review/reputation, LSA, SEO, paid ads, database reactivation, and sales coaching.
- Track margin by channel and cohort. A $297/month client from cold outbound has different payback than a paid-ad acquired client.
- Do not scale sales faster than delivery quality. The transcript's post-partnership emphasis on CSMs and SOPs is the strongest operational lesson.
- Design AI tooling around the CSM workflow: intake, site generation, edit preview, approval, publish, audit trail, rollback, and support escalation.

## Cautions

- Do not use the transcript's false-referral cold-call opener. It may book meetings, but it creates deception risk and trust debt.
- Do not convert "pressure until credit card or hang-up" into the sales process. The KB version should use diagnosis, proof, and clear commitment instead.
- Cold SMS, missed-call text-back, and lead follow-up need consent, opt-out, carrier, and TCPA analysis before implementation.
- The described review funnel sounds like review gating if it screens negative experiences away from public platforms. Use [[review-referral-automation]] guardrails instead.
- Profit, client count, ad spend, and exit-value claims are self-reported. Use them as hypotheses and benchmarks to investigate, not as proof.
- The AI delivery plan may hide real engineering complexity: template systems, hosting, edit safety, QA, local SEO metadata, form routing, and customer-specific exceptions.
