## TL;DR

A concise predecessor to Algorithms for Decision Making that helps ground planning and agent memory in probability, utility, MDPs, POMDPs, and belief updates.

## Key Claims

- Good decisions require modeling uncertainty explicitly rather than treating prediction as the end of the workflow.
- State uncertainty turns hidden-state inference into part of the control problem.
- Planning algorithms differ depending on whether model dynamics, state, and observations are known.
- Decision quality depends on utilities and downstream actions, not only prediction accuracy.
