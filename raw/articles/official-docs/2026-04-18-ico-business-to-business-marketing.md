## TL;DR

The ICO's B2B marketing guidance is a useful reminder that not all "business email" is regulated the same way. Under this guidance, unsolicited B2B electronic marketing to corporate subscribers can be permitted without prior consent under PECR, but the sender still must identify itself and provide a valid opt-out route.

## Key Claims

- Jurisdiction-specific direct-marketing rules matter for outbound systems.
- A corporate-subscriber exception does not remove identity and opt-out obligations.
- Suppression handling remains good practice even when consent is not required.

## Important Details

- The page says the PECR rule on direct marketing by electronic mail does not apply to corporate subscribers.
- It also says senders must not disguise or conceal their identity and must give a valid address for businesses to opt out or unsubscribe.
- The ICO recommends keeping a "do not email or text" list for corporate subscribers that object or opt out, and screening new B2B lists against it.
- The page emphasizes that "electronic mail" is interpreted broadly and covers more than only email in the narrow sense.

## My Notes

- This source is valuable because it pushes the KB away from US-only assumptions without pretending one jurisdiction's guidance generalizes globally.
