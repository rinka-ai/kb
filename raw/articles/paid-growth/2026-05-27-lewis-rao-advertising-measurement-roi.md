# The Unfavorable Economics of Measuring the Returns to Advertising

## TL;DR

Advertising incrementality is hard to measure precisely because individual outcomes are noisy relative to per-person ad cost. Even large field experiments can leave wide uncertainty around ROI.

## Key Claims

- The paper studies 25 large field experiments with major US retailers and brokerages.
- The experiments collectively represented millions of customers and significant digital advertising expenditure.
- The median confidence interval on ROI was very wide.
- Randomized control trials still improve the market by adding unbiased evidence, even when precision is expensive.

## Important Details

- The problem is not only bad tooling; it is statistical economics.
- Small effects, noisy purchase behavior, and low per-capita ad costs make precise ROI estimates difficult.
- Some firms need very large samples and long experiment windows to make confident budget decisions.
- This explains why platform dashboards can look decisive while causal evidence remains uncertain.
