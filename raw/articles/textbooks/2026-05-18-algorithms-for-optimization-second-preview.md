## TL;DR

An updated optimization reference that is especially useful for explaining automatic differentiation and the optimization machinery behind ML training and search.

## Key Claims

- Automatic differentiation is a central practical bridge between mathematical derivatives and trainable computational graphs.
- Gradient methods are only one part of a larger optimization landscape that includes bracketing, local descent, stochastic approximation, and constrained methods.
- Optimization algorithm choice should follow objective structure, derivative availability, noise, constraints, and evaluation cost.
- ML systems need optimization literacy because training, tuning, planning, and validation all become search problems.
