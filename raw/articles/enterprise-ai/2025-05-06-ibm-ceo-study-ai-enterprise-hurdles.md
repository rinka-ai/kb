# IBM Study: CEOs Double Down on AI While Navigating Enterprise Hurdles

## TL;DR

IBM supports the enterprise-infrastructure side of the thesis: CEOs are increasing AI investment, but many report disconnected technology, limited expected-ROI delivery, and low enterprise-wide scaling.

## Key Claims

- Surveyed CEOs expect AI investment growth to more than double over the next two years.
- Many CEOs report active adoption of AI agents and plans to implement them at scale.
- A large share identify integrated enterprise-wide data architecture as critical.
- Half of respondents acknowledge that recent investment pace has left disconnected, piecemeal technology.
- Only a minority of AI initiatives had delivered expected ROI or scaled enterprise-wide at the time of the study.

## Important Details

- The study surveyed 2,000 CEOs across 33 countries and 24 industries between February and April 2025.
- The source is useful for executive-level evidence of the gap between urgency and operating maturity.
- It links enterprise data architecture, collaboration across functions, and proprietary data to GenAI value capture.
