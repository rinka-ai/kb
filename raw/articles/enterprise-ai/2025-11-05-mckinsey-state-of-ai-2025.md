## TL;DR

McKinsey supports the core adoption/value gap: AI use is widespread, agent experimentation is common, but enterprise-level financial impact remains limited. High performers are distinguished by workflow redesign and stronger transformation practices.

## Key Claims

- Most organizations report regular AI use, but many are still experimenting or piloting rather than scaling.
- AI agents are widely being explored, but scaling remains concentrated in limited functions.
- Enterprise-wide EBIT impact is limited for most respondents.
- AI high performers are more likely to redesign workflows and set growth or innovation objectives alongside efficiency.
- Human validation processes, senior-leader ownership, KPI tracking, data infrastructure, and adoption practices correlate with value capture.

## Important Details

- The survey gathered 1,993 responses from 105 nations between June 25 and July 29, 2025.
- McKinsey defines high performers as respondents reporting significant value and at least 5 percent EBIT impact attributable to AI.
- Workflow redesign is identified as one of the strongest contributors to meaningful business impact among tested practices.
