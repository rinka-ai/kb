## TL;DR

A decision-theoretic bridge between probability, planning, RL, POMDPs, and multiagent systems; especially useful for grounding agent memory and planning in belief-state language.

## Key Claims

- Decision-making under uncertainty requires explicit models of probability, utility, observations, and sequential consequences.
- Bayesian networks, inference, and parameter learning provide the substrate for many decision algorithms.
- MDPs and POMDPs connect planning, value functions, policy search, and belief states.
- Multiagent reasoning adds strategic interaction, coordination, and state uncertainty beyond single-agent planning.
