# Google Ads policies: Misrepresentation

## TL;DR

Google's misrepresentation policy turns trust and claim accuracy into campaign infrastructure. Ads, offers, business information, billing details, and landing pages must line up.

## Key Claims

- Ads and destinations that deceive users or omit key information can violate Google Ads policy.
- Misrepresentation includes unavailable offers, hidden charges, unrealistic claims, false affiliation, phishing, and incomplete business details.
- Google explicitly cares about consistency between ad and landing page or app.
- Policy enforcement can interrupt campaigns regardless of creative performance.

## Important Details

- Ecommerce and lead generation are especially exposed because checkout, shipping, refund, contact, and billing details must match user expectations.
- Copywriting should avoid unrealistic financial, weight-loss, health, or outcome claims.
- Landing pages need visible trust information such as contact, terms, privacy, pricing, and offer conditions where relevant.
- Misrepresentation risk increases when AI-generated copy exaggerates benefits or invents unavailable offers.
