# FTC: Endorsement Guides - What People Are Asking

## TL;DR

UGC, influencer, testimonial, and review-based ads need disclosure discipline. A platform's built-in tool may not be sufficient by itself if the material connection is not clear and conspicuous to the audience.

## Key Claims

- The FTC's Endorsement Guides were revised in 2023 with new principles, examples, and definitions.
- Material connections between endorsers and brands should be clearly and conspicuously disclosed.
- The FTC evaluates whether a disclosure is actually clear in context, including social media formats.
- Companies that pre-approve influencer posts should review them for truth-in-advertising and disclosure responsibilities.

## Important Details

- A personalized discount code may signal some relationship, but may not fully clarify the financial relationship.
- Platform disclosure tools can help, but the legal question is whether the audience understands the relevant connection.
- Reviews, testimonials, and creator claims must reflect genuine experience and substantiated claims.
- Disclosure should be built into creator briefs, approval flows, whitelisting, partnership ads, and paid usage rights.
