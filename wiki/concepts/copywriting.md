# Copywriting

## Summary

Copywriting is not wordsmithing after the strategy is done. In paid growth, copy is the compression layer between customer research and action. It names the problem, clarifies the offer, answers objections, proves credibility, and tells the buyer what to do next.

The durable copywriting equation:

customer language + offer truth + proof + channel context + compliance = usable copy.

## Research Inputs

The strongest copy starts from voice-of-customer research:

- reviews
- customer interviews
- surveys
- sales-call transcripts
- support tickets
- social comments
- forum discussions
- competitor ad comments
- objections from lost deals

The goal is to extract pain, desired outcomes, triggers, alternatives, objections, and words customers naturally use.

## Channel Differences

Search ads respond to declared intent. The query already tells you what the person wants or suspects. Copy should be direct, relevant, and modular.

Paid social interrupts the feed. The copy must create context quickly: why this problem matters, why now, why this solution is credible, and why the next step is worth taking.

UGC scripts need to sound spoken. The copywriter's job is to provide structure and raw material, then let the creator make it believable.

Owned content carries the depth. Ads can make claims compact, but landing pages and content must substantiate them.

## Asset Taxonomy

A useful copy library separates:

- problem statements
- outcome statements
- mechanism explanations
- objection answers
- proof snippets
- comparison lines
- urgency lines
- audience call-outs
- CTAs
- disclaimers and required claims
- hooks
- landing-page section headlines

This taxonomy maps cleanly to Google RSA headlines, Meta primary text, UGC hooks, overlays, email subjects, and landing-page modules.

## Policy-Safe Copy

Good copy is specific without becoming deceptive. Avoid:

- diagnosing the viewer
- implying sensitive personal attributes
- guaranteeing outcomes
- hiding conditions or costs
- inventing proof
- overstating typical results
- using undisclosed endorsements
- creating ad-to-page mismatch

Policy-safe copy is not bland. It simply shifts from "You are X and this will fix you" to "For people dealing with Y, here is a way to do Z, with proof A and condition B."

## AI Copy

AI can generate variants quickly and platforms are increasingly optimizing generated copy with performance feedback. This increases throughput but also increases governance needs.

AI copy should be checked against:

- source customer language
- offer accuracy
- substantiation
- platform policy
- landing-page match
- brand voice
- qualified conversion quality
