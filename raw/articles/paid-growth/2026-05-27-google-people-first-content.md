# Google Search Central: Creating helpful, reliable, people-first content

## TL;DR

Content creation should start with audience usefulness and first-hand expertise, not with search-volume arbitrage. Google's guidance is a corrective against thin AI-scaled content that summarizes others without adding value.

## Key Claims

- Google's ranking systems prioritize helpful, reliable information created to benefit people.
- Content should have an intended audience, first-hand expertise, a clear site purpose, and enough substance to help the reader achieve their goal.
- Search-engine-first warning signs include mass automation, chasing unrelated topics, summarizing others without added value, and arbitrary word-count targets.
- SEO is useful when applied to people-first content rather than used as the primary reason for publishing.

## Important Details

- Google explicitly says there is no preferred word count.
- It warns against changing dates to appear fresh without substantive updates.
- It connects helpfulness with E-E-A-T: experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness.
- The guidance applies to owned content that paid campaigns often amplify or send traffic toward.
