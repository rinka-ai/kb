# Google Ads: Best practices for asset groups in Performance Max campaigns

## TL;DR

Asset groups are the thematic units of Performance Max creative. They should contain enough text, images, logos, and videos for Google AI to assemble ads across channels while staying coherent around a product, theme, language, or audience.

## Key Claims

- Google AI assembles assets into formats and serves the most relevant creative for the campaign goal.
- Each asset group should cover text, image, and video asset types.
- Advertisers should use Ad Strength, quality guidelines, and variations to improve asset groups.
- Google recommends waiting long enough before judging asset performance.

## Important Details

- Recommended text assets include up to 15 headlines, 5 descriptions, long headlines, business name, CTA, final URL, and display URL paths.
- Recommended image assets include multiple horizontal, square, and vertical images plus square and horizontal logos.
- Recommended video coverage includes horizontal, vertical, and square assets.
- If advertisers do not add video, Google may auto-generate videos from supplied assets.
- Google recommends allowing roughly 2-3 weeks before replacing low-performing assets.

## My Notes

- Asset groups are concept packs. They should map to distinct customer jobs, product lines, messages, or market segments rather than arbitrary media buckets.
- The auto-generated video warning matters: if video quality is strategically important, upload real vertical/square/horizontal video instead of letting the system improvise.
