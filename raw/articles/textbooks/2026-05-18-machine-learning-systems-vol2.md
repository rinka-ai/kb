A large-scale ML systems text that explains why distributed AI engineering is dominated by compute allocation, communication, reliability, governance, energy, and fleet-level constraints.

## Key Claims

- At scale, communication, failure, and governance become load-bearing system properties.
- Scaling laws are useful operational tools but have cost, sustainability, and breakdown conditions.
- Distributed efficiency depends on more than adding hardware; the architecture must manage communication and reliability.
- ML governance belongs inside system design because model behavior, data, deployment, and infrastructure interact.
