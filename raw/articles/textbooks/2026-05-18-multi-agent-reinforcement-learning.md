A modern MARL text that separates game-theoretic multi-agent learning from LLM-agent orchestration and gives the KB a stronger technical meaning of multi-agent systems.

## Key Claims

- Multi-agent learning is harder than single-agent RL because other agents make the environment nonstationary and strategic.
- Games, stochastic games, and partially observable stochastic games are core models of interaction.
- Coordination, communication, credit assignment, and decentralized execution are central MARL design problems.
- Evaluation needs learning curves and interaction-aware baselines, not only single-agent success metrics.
