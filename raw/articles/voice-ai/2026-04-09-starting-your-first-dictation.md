Available on: Mac, Windows, iOS, Android
Whether you're composing an email, writing a message, or drafting a document — this guide gets you dictating in under a minute. Hold your hotkey, speak, and release.

Click into any text field (Slack, Google Docs, Notion, etc.) so you see a flashing cursor.
Press and hold the Fn key (the default hotkey). When you hear a ping or see the white bars moving, start speaking.
Speak your message naturally at a steady pace. Flow listens while you hold the hotkey.
Release the hotkey when you're done. Flow pastes your formatted text into the text field.

If your text appears in the field, your first dictation is complete.
Note: If you tap the hotkey instead of holding it, a reminder appears showing your configured shortcut — for example, "Don't tap. Hold down Fn." This shows up to 3 times. If your keyboard doesn't have an Fn key, Flow automatically assigns Ctrl + Opt as your default hotkey during setup (with Ctrl + Opt + Space for hands-free mode).
Tip: Press Esc while dictating to cancel. If you spoke for at least half a second, the text can be found in Recent activity.

Click into any text field (Slack, Google Docs, Notion, etc.) so you see a flashing cursor.
Press and hold Ctrl + Win (the default hotkey). When you hear a ping or see the white bars moving, start speaking.
Speak your message naturally at a steady pace. Flow listens while you hold the hotkey.
Release the hotkey when you're done. Flow pastes your formatted text into the text field.

If your text appears in the field, your first dictation is complete.
Note: If you tap the hotkey instead of holding it, a reminder appears showing your configured shortcut — for example, "Don't tap. Hold down Ctrl+Win." This shows up to 3 times.
Tip: Press Esc while dictating to cancel. The text won't paste, but you can find it in Recent activity.

Open the Wispr Flow app on your iPhone or iPad and sign in if prompted.
Complete the onboarding tutorial if you haven't already — you'll practice dictating during the guided setup.
Open a note, document, or text field and tap into it so you see the cursor.
Tap the microphone button. If prompted, tap Allow to grant microphone access. When Flow is ready, you'll see "Say something!"
Speak your message at a steady pace. Flow shows your words as it transcribes.
Tap the check button when done. Your formatted text appears in the text field.

If your text appears, your first dictation is complete.
Tip: A style pill appears on the keyboard next to the "Start Flow" button showing your current writing style. Tap it to temporarily switch styles. Manage your default style from Settings → Personalization.
Tip: Tap cancel to stop without inserting text. You can still find past transcripts in Recent activity.

On Android, the Flow Bubble is a floating overlay that appears over all apps when you tap into a text field.

Open the Wispr Flow app and complete onboarding. Grant the required permissions when prompted (microphone, accessibility service, and display over other apps).
Tap into any text field in any app. The Flow Bubble appears on the edge of your screen.
Start dictating using one of two modes:

Tap-to-Dictate: Tap the bubble once. The bubble expands to show Cancel (X), a waveform, and Done (checkmark) buttons. Speak your message, then tap the checkmark when finished.
Hold-to-Dictate: Press and hold the bubble. Speak while holding — the bubble shows a waveform with a pulsing recording indicator. Release when done.

Wait briefly while Flow transcribes. Your formatted text is inserted into the text field.

If your text appears, your first dictation is complete.
Note: If Flow cannot verify that text was successfully inserted, a copy button appears next to the Flow Bubble. Tap it to copy your transcribed text to the clipboard. The button auto-dismisses after a few seconds or when you start a new dictation.
Tip: Drag the Flow Bubble anywhere on screen — it snaps to the left or right edge. Drag it to the snooze circle at the bottom to hide it for 10 minutes. End snooze early by shaking your phone while in a text field, or tapping "End snooze now" on the Home screen.

Can I use a mouse button instead of the keyboard hotkey?

Yes, on Mac and Windows. Open Settings → General → Shortcuts and assign a mouse button as your trigger. Supported buttons include Middle Click, Mouse 4–Mouse 10, and Aux Button (some Logitech mice). You can also combine a keyboard key with a mouse button (e.g., Ctrl + Mouse 4).
Left and right click cannot be used as triggers. Mouse 6–10 may need to be bound via the native system helper rather than clicking in the Settings UI.

Mac: Press Fn + Space to start hands-free mode. Speak without holding any key. When done, press Fn to stop and paste.
Windows: Press Ctrl + Win + Space to start hands-free mode. Speak without holding any key. When done, press Ctrl + Win to stop and paste.

Can I start dictation from the Flow Bar?
Yes, on desktop. Click the Flow Bar in the center of your screen. Wait for the ping or moving white bars, then start speaking. When you stop, Flow pastes your text into the active text field. The Flow Bar may not be visible on new installations — to show it, go to Settings and enable it.

Will my text always go into the right field?
Yes. Flow tracks which text field you clicked into when you started dictating and always inserts your text there — even in apps with several input fields on screen at once, like chat apps or Google Gemini.

Why do I see a "Flow was processing your last transcript" message?
This appears when your previous dictation is still being transcribed. Flow won't start a new session until the current one finishes. Dismiss the notification, then press your shortcut again. You can also click the Cancel button in the notification to cancel the in-progress transcription.

What are Smart Formatting and Backtrack?

Smart Formatting automatically formats your dictation — say "first, second" to create a numbered list, or "new line" to insert a paragraph break.
Backtrack lets you change your mind mid-sentence and Flow adjusts what you just said.

On desktop, saying "press enter" at the end of your dictation tells Flow to press the Enter key after your text is pasted. The words "press enter" are removed from the output.
The first time you use this command, a notification explains the feature (the Enter key is not pressed on that first use). Enable or disable the feature anytime under Settings → Experimental.

Desktop (Mac/Windows): Sessions automatically end after 20 minutes. A warning notification appears at 19 minutes with a Recover text button so you can retrieve what you said.
iOS: Sessions have a 5-minute maximum.
Android: Sessions have a 5-minute maximum. You'll feel a vibration and see a warning at the 4-minute mark. At 5 minutes, the session ends and your text is submitted.

Why does my dictation output sound different depending on which app I'm using (Android)?
This is intentional. With Personalization Styles (available in Android version 1.4.0 and later), Flow automatically applies the writing style you've chosen for each app category. For example, dictating in a work app produces more formal output than dictating in a personal messaging app. Set your preferred style for each category in Personalization Styles in the Flow app.

Clipboard usage (Mac/Windows): Flow temporarily uses your clipboard to paste text, but saves and restores your previous clipboard contents afterward.
Manual paste fallback (Mac/Windows): If Flow cannot paste text automatically (e.g., the target app doesn't accept the paste), a notification prompts you to click a text field and use your Paste Last Transcript shortcut (default: Cmd+Ctrl+V on Mac, Shift+Alt+Z on Windows). Your dictated text remains on the clipboard.
Screen timeout (iOS/Android): While you're actively dictating, your screen stays on. Normal screen timeout behavior resumes once dictation ends.
Mouse button triggers (Mac/Windows): When a mouse button is assigned as a dictation trigger, it no longer performs its default action in other apps (e.g., Mouse 4 won't trigger browser back navigation). Left and right click cannot be used as triggers.
Writing styles (iOS): Your default writing style varies by app category (Personal, Work, Email, Other). Temporarily override it using the style pill on the keyboard — the override expires 15 minutes after your first dictation using that style. Manage your default style from Settings → Personalization. Writing styles are available in English (US and British) only.
Writing styles (Android): On Android 1.4.0 and later, Flow automatically applies a writing style based on which app you're dictating in. Set a preferred style for each app category in the Flow app. Available styles vary by category: Personal apps offer Formal, Casual, and Very Casual; Work, Email, and Other apps offer Formal, Casual, and Excited. Writing styles currently apply in English only.
Animated glow ring (iOS): If you're using the Flow QWERTY keyboard, a purple spinning glow ring appears around the "Start Flow" button. The animation is suppressed if "Reduce Motion" is turned on in your device's accessibility settings.
Android permissions: Flow requires three permissions: Microphone (to hear you), Accessibility Service (to insert text), and Display over other apps (to show the Flow Bubble). If any permission is missing, the Home screen shows an error card guiding you to enable it.
Android battery optimization: For best reliability, allow Wispr Flow to ignore battery optimization. This keeps the Flow Bubble ready when you need it. Non-Pixel devices may see additional manufacturer-specific battery optimization prompts.
Android banking apps: The Flow Bubble hides in banking and financial apps for security. Shake-to-unsnooze is also blocked while a banking app is in the foreground.

The following issues have been resolved in recent versions. If you're experiencing any of them, update Wispr Flow to the latest version and restart the app.

Background tasks interfered with active dictation sessions (Mac/Windows): Background tasks could occasionally run during an active dictation session, causing slowdowns or interruptions — particularly on M1 and M2 Macs. Background tasks are now automatically paused while you're dictating and resume once your session ends.
MacBook users with auto-start at login were assigned the wrong default shortcut (Mac): If Flow started before you unlocked your screen, it could incorrectly detect that your keyboard doesn't have an Fn key and assign Ctrl + Opt as your default shortcut instead of Fn. If this happened to you, go to Settings → General → Shortcuts to switch back to the Fn key.
Dictation bubble displayed incorrectly after switching modes (Android): Switching between Hold-to-Dictate and Tap-to-Dictate could cause the bubble UI to show the wrong size or clip the Done (checkmark) button off-screen. The dictation bubble now fully resets between sessions.
App crashed when adding a duplicate word to the dictionary (Android): If the same word already existed in the dictionary, adding it again could cause the app to crash. Fixed in Android version 1.4.0.
App crashed when deleting a transcript on the Home Screen (Android): Deleting a transcript from the Home Screen could cause the app to crash unexpectedly. Fixed in Android version 1.4.0.
App crashed during screen transitions (Android): Navigating between screens could occasionally cause the app to crash. Fixed in Android version 1.4.0.
Words in transcriptions were sometimes misread (Android): An error in how speech recognition processed individual words could produce incorrect output. Fixed in Android version 1.4.0.
Dictation activation delayed or missed during normal typing (Mac/Windows): Pressing the hotkey while typing could occasionally cause dictation to start several seconds late or not start at all. Keyboard shortcuts such as dictation activation, transform shortcuts (e.g., Opt+S), and paste last text were affected.
Keyboard writing style didn't apply on iOS: Selecting a temporary writing style using the style pill on the keyboard sometimes had no effect on the dictation output.
Hotkey took 30+ seconds to respond on Mac: Pressing the activation shortcut could cause a long delay before dictation started.
Keyboard layout flashed on iOS: Tapping the mic button inside the Wispr Flow app briefly showed the keyboard layout before the waveform appeared.
Dictation timed out on overheating iPhone: When a device was running hot, stopping dictation could time out before processing finished.
Dictation wouldn't start after cancelling a session: Cancelling a dictation session while it was still starting up could leave Flow in a stuck state.
Text appeared in all lowercase on Windows: A capitalization bug during onboarding and in-app dictation caused all text to appear in lowercase.
