## TL;DR

AkitaOnRails is a long-running practitioner archive whose durable themes are anti-cargo-cult engineering, software economics, operational ownership, communication discipline, and skepticism toward hype. The English edition currently spans 363 dated posts across 2006-2018 and 2025-2026, making it useful as a collection-level source for Akita's engineering worldview and its application to Rails, Linux, self-hosting, and AI coding agents.

## Key Claims

- The archive is better understood as a longitudinal practitioner record than as a narrow framework blog.
- The strongest recurring ideas are that tools change faster than engineering habits, process cannot replace competent practitioners, and production responsibility is part of software development.
- The 2025-2026 AI/agent phase extends earlier Akita themes rather than replacing them: the same anti-hype and economics-first worldview is now applied to LLMs, coding agents, RAG, and sandboxing.
- The English archive is heterogeneous and partly worldview-oriented, with a large off-topic and interview component in addition to technical implementation posts.

- The archive mix is broad:
  - 95 titles explicitly marked off-topic
  - 22 interview posts beginning with "Chatting with"
  - 10 translation posts
- Major phase shifts visible in the English corpus:
  - 2006-2009: Rails/Ruby evangelism, interviews, translations, and early worldview writing
  - 2010-2014: management, estimation, agility, startup realism, and communication failures
  - 2015-2018: Elixir/Phoenix, Crystal, Linux, GitLab, self-hosting, deployment, and production Rails
  - 2025-2026: local AI, coding agents, model comparison, agent security, home-lab infrastructure, and high-speed product iteration
- Representative anchor posts across phases include:

## My Notes

- This is a collection-level source note, not a single-post ingest. Its purpose is to anchor provenance for archive-wide synthesis.
