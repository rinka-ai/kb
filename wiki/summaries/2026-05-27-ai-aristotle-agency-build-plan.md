# AI Aristotle Agency Build Plan

## Summary

The JP Middleton video is valuable for the agency we want to build because it describes a coherent productized offer: reactivate existing leads, improve review/referral loops, recover inbound demand quickly, coach sales calls, then scale paid ads. The KB should keep this as a working plan, but the plan only becomes real after we validate a niche, compliance path, delivery stack, and our own evidence.

The core sections have now been split into durable topic pages: [[local-business-ai-acquisition-system]], [[database-reactivation]], [[review-referral-automation]], [[speed-to-lead-and-missed-call-recovery]], [[sales-coaching-gpt]], [[ai-agency-sales-process]], and [[ai-agency-paid-ads-process]].

## What To Keep

- One niche first. The model depends on repeatability, so broad "AI automation agency" positioning is too loose.
- One integrated offer. The pitch should be a revenue system, not separate bots.
- Sequence matters. Earn trust and working capital before asking clients to spend more on ads.
- Sell by diagnosis. The call should quantify the buyer's funnel gap before prescribing the system.
- Use AI for leverage, but sell outcomes. The buyer cares about booked appointments, revenue, reviews, referrals, and staff time, not the model.

## What To Validate

- Niche economics: LTV, gross margin, sales cycle, owner accessibility, current ad spend, and staffing constraints.
- Data rights: whether old leads can legally and ethically be contacted through SMS, phone, email, or another channel.
- Review workflow: whether feedback, review requests, incentives, and referral raffles comply with platform and consumer-protection rules.
- Delivery reliability: CRM import/export, calendar booking, message routing, opt-out handling, escalation, and reporting.
- Paid acquisition: whether the agency can acquire clients profitably in the niche before hiring or overbuilding.
- Attribution: whether observed client revenue can be credibly tied to the agency system.

## External Research Checks

- [Lead Response Management Study](https://onecavo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/MIT-InsideSales.com_Lead-Response-Management.pdf) supports the broad speed-to-lead thesis: fast follow-up strongly improves contact and qualification odds. Use it as a hypothesis, then validate against the first niche's own leads.
- [Harvard Business School: The Short Life of Online Sales Leads](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=39955) confirms the HBR source record behind the speed-to-lead citation.
- [InsideSales/XANT 2021 lead response research](https://www.insidesales.com/response-time-matters/) adds a newer vendor dataset supporting the same response-speed hypothesis.
- [BrightLocal Local Consumer Review Survey 2026](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey/) supports the reputation-management pillar: consumers still heavily use local reviews, and many customers will review when asked.
- [Google Business Profile: local ranking](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091/improve-your-local-ranking-on-google) supports complete profiles, review count, positive ratings, and prominence as relevant to local visibility.
- [Google Business Profile: get reviews](https://support.google.com/business/answer/3474122/get-reviews-on-google) and [Google prohibited content policy](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7400114) create the review-system guardrail: ask real customers, do not incentivize reviews, do not gate for positive sentiment, and do not request specific content.
- [FTC: Soliciting and Paying for Online Reviews](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/soliciting-paying-online-reviews-guide-marketers) adds the federal deception guardrail: no non-customer reviews, no likely-positive-only solicitation, no positivity-conditioned incentives, and disclose incentives where allowed.
- [FTC: Consumer Reviews and Testimonials Rule Q&A](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/consumer-reviews-testimonials-rule-questions-answers) strengthens the review guardrail because agencies and reputation vendors can be liable for fake reviews, sentiment-conditioned incentives, review suppression, and fake indicators of influence.
- [FTC: Follow the Lead](https://www.ftc.gov/reports/staff-perspective-follow-lead) makes lead buying/reactivation a data-governance issue, not just a marketing tactic.
- [FCC AI voice TCPA ruling](https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/FCC-24-17A1.pdf), the FCC's [TCPA consent FAQ](https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-408396A1.pdf), and [FCC DA 25-621](https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-25-621A1.pdf) clarify the current telecom constraint: the stricter one-to-one consent rule was vacated/deleted, but AI voice calls and telemarketing texts/calls remain consent-sensitive under TCPA.
- [NIST AI RMF 1.0](https://www.nist.gov/publications/artificial-intelligence-risk-management-framework-ai-rmf-10) anchors the sales-coaching GPT as an AI risk/evaluation workflow, not just a prompt.
- [Salesforce State of Sales 2026](https://www.salesforce.com/en-us/wp-content/uploads/sites/4/documents/reports/sales/salesforce-state-of-sales-report-2026.pdf) supports the relevance of coaching agents, roleplay, data hygiene, and security concerns, but is vendor survey evidence.
- [HBR: The End of Solution Sales](https://hbr.org/2012/07/the-end-of-solution-sales) supports consultative/diagnostic selling as a business-sales pattern, but it is not proof of Middleton's close-rate claims.
- [Meta Advertising Standards](https://transparency.meta.com/policies/ad-standards/) is the official paid-social policy anchor; ad claims, personal attributes, landing pages, and business assets can all be reviewed.
- [Lewis and Rao, QJE: The Unfavorable Economics of Measuring the Returns to Advertising](https://doi.org/10.1093/qje/qjv023) is the measurement caution: even experiments can leave ROI uncertainty, so platform dashboards are not enough.

## Next Experiments

- Pick three candidate niches and score them against LTV, urgency, existing lead databases, owner reachability, compliance complexity, and likely proof speed.
- Draft a compliant reactivation script and review/referral script before choosing tools.
- Build a minimal dashboard schema before automation so every pilot has the same evidence trail.
- Run the first offer as a controlled pilot, not as a fully proven $10,000/month package.
