# Local Business AI Acquisition System

## Summary

The durable lesson from the Middleton "AI Aristotle" offer is not that every agency should copy a gym-agency script. It is that a local business usually leaks revenue across several connected places: old leads are untouched, reviews are inconsistent, inbound leads are answered too slowly, calls are missed, salespeople improvise, and ads scale the mess. The stronger offer is one sequenced acquisition workflow that fixes the leak order before adding more spend.

This should be treated as a validation-backed operating model, not as audited proof. Middleton's numbers and client results are self-reported, and the Pavlo website-agency interview adds more self-reported operator metrics rather than audited evidence. The useful KB abstraction is the system design: one vertical, one measurable funnel, one dashboard, one set of SOPs, and one evidence trail.

## Operating Model

- Start with existing assets before buying more traffic: old leads, former customers, current customers, review opportunities, and referral relationships.
- Improve trust and conversion before scaling demand: reviews, referrals, speed-to-lead, appointment reminders, and sales coaching all make paid ads less wasteful.
- Sell one system, not five disconnected services. The buyer should experience one commercial promise and one dashboard even if delivery has multiple modules.
- Map every module to a funnel metric: booked appointments, showed appointments, close rate, revenue collected, reviews earned, referrals generated, ad cost per booked appointment, CAC, payback, and retention.
- Keep the system verticalized. A gym, dentist, med spa, HVAC contractor, landscaper, insurer, and generator installer have different value, sales cycle, compliance, and proof requirements.

## Core Pillars

- [[database-reactivation]]: re-engage dormant leads, former customers, or old prospects only after consent and data quality are verified.
- [[review-referral-automation]]: turn real customer interactions into feedback, public reviews, and referrals without deceptive gating or undisclosed incentives.
- [[speed-to-lead-and-missed-call-recovery]]: answer web leads and missed calls quickly, book/reschedule appointments, and route exceptions to humans.
- [[sales-coaching-gpt]]: use transcripts and a rubric to coach staff against a standard consultation process.
- [[ai-agency-paid-ads-process]]: scale demand after the business can follow up, show, close, and attribute the leads being generated.
- [[ai-agency-sales-process]]: sell the system by diagnosing the buyer's funnel economics before prescribing the offer.

The Pavlo source shows a lower-friction entry offer for contractors: website, missed-call text-back, form follow-up, review requests, and Google profile help at roughly $297/month. This is not the whole acquisition system, but it can be a wedge into the same funnel if the agency later adds reactivation, paid ads, sales coaching, and stronger measurement.

## Implementation Sequence

1. Pick one niche and write the buyer-economics brief: LTV, gross margin, buying trigger, staffing pattern, current ad spend, common CRM/calendar tools, and regulatory surface.
2. Audit the current funnel numerically: lead volume, response time, missed calls, booked appointments, show rate, close rate, review volume, referral flow, revenue, and ad spend.
3. Verify data rights before reactivation: source of lead, timestamp, consent language, channel permission, opt-out state, suppression list, and jurisdiction.
4. Choose the first wedge deliberately: either reactivation plus speed-to-lead for fast proof, or a cheap website/reputation/follow-up package if the buyer mainly needs trust and basic capture infrastructure.
5. Add review/referral and sales-coaching loops only when the business can handle the operational follow-through.
6. Launch paid ads with a clean landing page, booking path, follow-up path, CRM tracking, and outcome dashboard.
7. Convert exceptions into SOP decisions: reject custom work, price it separately, or turn it into a reusable module.

## Dashboard Spine

- Existing assets: eligible contacts, contacted contacts, replies, booked appointments, shows, sales, revenue, opt-outs, complaints.
- Reputation/referrals: review requests, public reviews, rating movement, review replies, referrals requested, referral leads, referral sales.
- Response: median first response time, missed-call recovery rate, booked appointment rate, reschedule rate, no-show rate, human handoff rate.
- Sales: script adherence, discovery completeness, objections, close rate, revenue per show, follow-up completion.
- Paid acquisition: spend, CPM/CPC/CPL, booked appointment cost, show cost, sales cost, CAC, payback, retained revenue.

## Guardrails

- Do not make ad or sales claims from another niche sound like proof for the buyer's niche.
- Do not treat old contact information as consent for automated texts or calls without evidence.
- Do not copy review-gating tactics from the transcript without checking Google, FTC, platform, and local rules.
- Do not let paid lead volume become the success metric. Revenue and retention matter more than cheap leads.
- Do not sell the whole system as proven until at least one narrow pilot has produced defensible evidence in the target niche.

## Evidence And Authority

- Source claim status: the Middleton transcript is operator evidence and should be treated as self-reported, not independently verified proof.
- Review/reputation authority: [Google Business Profile local-ranking guidance](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091/improve-your-local-ranking-on-google) supports complete profiles, review responses, and review/prominence relevance, but does not provide a simple ranking formula.
- Review-policy authority: [Google prohibited and restricted content](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7400114) and [FTC review-solicitation guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/soliciting-paying-online-reviews-guide-marketers) are stronger than the transcript for review/referral workflow design.
- Review-demand evidence: [BrightLocal's Local Consumer Review Survey 2026](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey/) is a high-quality practitioner survey supporting the commercial importance of reviews, but it does not override Google or FTC policy.
- Lead/contact authority: [FTC lead-generation guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/reports/follow-lead-workshop-staff-perspective), [FTC CAN-SPAM guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business), [FCC TCPA AI voice guidance](https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/FCC-24-17A1.pdf), and the FCC's [TCPA consent FAQ](https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-408396A1.pdf) make data provenance, consent, opt-out, and AI voice/text use first-class design constraints.
- Speed-to-lead evidence: Harvard Business School's record for HBR's [The Short Life of Online Sales Leads](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=39955), the [MIT/InsideSales lead response study](https://onecavo.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/MIT-InsideSales.com_Lead-Response-Management.pdf), and InsideSales/XANT's [2021 lead response research](https://www.insidesales.com/response-time-matters/) are useful directional evidence, but they should be validated against each niche's own inbound leads.
- Paid-growth authority: [[2026-05-27-meta-performance-5]], [[2026-05-27-meta-advertising-standards]], and [[2026-05-27-meta-conversions-api]] are the official Meta anchors; [[2026-05-27-lewis-rao-advertising-measurement-roi]] is the measurement-skepticism anchor.
