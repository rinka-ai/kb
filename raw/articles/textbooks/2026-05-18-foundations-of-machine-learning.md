## TL;DR

A theory-first ML text that explains why empirical performance needs generalization guarantees, how hypothesis classes can be controlled, and how regularization and kernels connect optimization with statistical guarantees.

## Key Claims

- Generalization is the central ML problem: training loss alone is not enough to explain performance on new data.
- PAC-style framing makes assumptions, sample size, hypothesis class, and confidence explicit.
- Complexity measures such as VC dimension and Rademacher complexity explain why richer model classes need stronger control.
- Model selection and regularization are not just engineering tricks; they mediate the estimation/approximation tradeoff.
