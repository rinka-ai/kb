# AI Agency Meta Ad Checklist

## Summary

For an AI agency selling to local businesses, a good Meta ad should answer five questions fast: who is this for, what is new about the mechanism, what business outcome does it create, why should I trust it, and what should I do next? The user-provided screenshots are useful as tactical execution checklists, but they should be filtered through Meta policy, substantiation, and landing-page match.

The four visible ad surfaces each need a job: copy creates relevance, creative stops the scroll and makes the AI mechanism visible, the headline compresses urgency and niche fit, and the description de-risks the click with proof.

## Usable Pattern

- Primary text: call out the niche in the first visible line before the "See More" cutoff.
- Mechanism: make the AI angle legible in plain language, but only name "ChatGPT" or a plugin if that is true.
- Outcome: lead with booked appointments, follow-up handled, workload reduced, or ROI only when the claim can be defended.
- Reading level: keep copy and overlays simple enough for quick feed scanning.
- Creative: use text overlay to name the market, add niche-relevant symbols, and use high contrast so the offer is readable at a glance.
- Headline: combine "new" or another freshness cue with the niche and the specific market-fit offer.
- Description: use the description as a proof/trust layer, not a second pitch, when the placement shows it.

## Production Checklist

Before launching a variant, collect:

- target niche and the exact buyer role
- one appointment, lead, revenue, or time-saving outcome
- the real AI mechanism in non-technical language
- proof available now: case study, pilot result, testimonial, demo, benchmark, or transparent absence of proof
- claim boundaries: what cannot be promised yet
- landing-page section that substantiates the exact ad promise
- fallback version without ROI/success claims if proof is thin

## Variant Matrix

Create at least three versions from the same offer:

- proof-led: starts with a real result or case study
- mechanism-led: explains the new AI follow-up or booking workflow
- labor-saving: emphasizes fewer manual follow-ups and less admin work

Each version should keep the same niche, offer, and CTA so test results reflect the angle rather than random copy changes.

## Guardrails

- Do not imply sensitive personal attributes in Meta copy.
- Do not use "ChatGPT Plugin" as a buzzword if the product is not actually a plugin.
- Do not guarantee ROI, leads, appointments, or revenue.
- Do not borrow success stories from another niche as if they apply to the current market.
- Do not let high-contrast creative become cluttered or misleading.
- Do not send clicks to a landing page that fails to prove the same claim.
